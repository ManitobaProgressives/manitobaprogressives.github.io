Goals

Focus On The Working Class


Canada has seen two "once in a lifetime" economic crashes in the last 13 years. The working class each time got poorer while the rich got richer.


Our focus is providing social support systems, living wages, and more labour rights to ensure those who work to earn money, earn their value in labour.


  • Create new government jobs to empty the reserve of unemployed peoples
  • Raise minimum wage to $25.00 per hour - bring it back to 1974 rates after inflation
  • Legislate a 32 hour work week with no reduction of weekly gross income
  • Ban contracts, gig work, and other precarious workplace employment policies
  • Mandate a 3:1 Full time to Part time employment pattern
  • Enforce unions to mediate and control workplace relations between employee and employer
  • Enforce private sector wage transparency in the same manner public sector wage transparency is provided
  • Enforce long term repercussions to employers whom are responsible for employee workplace fatalities
  • Enforce a paid sick leave policy to allow all workers to better their health and quality of life

Greater Living Standards

Healthcare in Manitoba has been in the decline. Hospitals are being closed across the province while nurses are being burnt out due to negligence from a careless Conservative government.

Canadian lives matter equally. Our focus for greater living standards is to make actions to prove this. We will provide to all Manitobans without prejudice coverage for everyone's health and well being:


  • Provide 100% Medical Coverage for all prescription drugs, for all dental care, for all optical care, for all mental healthcare
  • Provide a Universal Childcare coverage for all Manitobans
  • Provide a monthly Universal Basic Income to all adult Manitobans
  • Guarantee the right to organize, to safely collaborate, strike, and protest without criminal punishment
  • Create an organ donor program in which if new or existing medical emergencies do arise, organ donors get priority access to medical supplies and treatments

Making Active Changes For A Green New Deal

Climate change is an issue that affects us all in some form or another. It is not only to our own benefit but also to those future generations to build a green environment without the need of fossil fuels.


Manitoba needs more action taken to introduce renewable energy sources to homes and businesses, as well as actions taken to reduce and reuse waste before resorting to the final option of recycling.


  • Provide reimbursements to apartment tenants, apartment owners, and condo owners, and home owners to convert from harmful energy sources to new green energy sources
  • Provide reimbursements to private sector companies for converting from harmful energy sources to new green energy sources
  • Install wind farms and solar panel farms across the prairies to benefit from our plentiful natural resources
  • Introduce nuclear energy sources as a tertiary source of power in locations this would benefit
  • Work towards reducing the cost of green energy bills among Manitoban residents
  • Create incentives to purchase electric vehicles for Manitoban residents and businesses
  • Ban the sale of fossil fuel vehicles by 2025

Housing Rights

Every Manitoban deserves a roof over their heads when they go to sleep.
Every Manitoban deserves a place they can call home.


We will focus on building new infrastructure to meet the growing demands of new members to our communities.


  • Build 1500 homes in rural Manitoba with the focus of affordability
  • Build a mix of 2000 homes and housing complexes in Winnipeg both for first time home buyers as well as to address the homeless population
  • Convert private sector apartment owners from a for profit motive to a for populous well-being motive
  • Create policy to dynamically build housing infrastructure to the rate of those seeking home ownership

Pandemic Recovery & Proactive Measures

Manitobans depending on a government to save them but during the COVID-19 pandemic the Conservatives failed them time and time again. Conservatives blamed the Manitobans for doing what they were told.


The government should do better, can do better for its people.


  • Ensure all of Manitobans have vaccines approved and distributed by Canadian Federal Government bodies.
  • Ensure all Manitobans regardless of age, skin color, sex, or any other factor have greater access to past, present, and future vaccines
  • Create a pandemic response department to prepare, develop, and implement plans of engagement in medical emergency crisis
  • Create policies to better engage with Canadian Federal Government bodies so that there is greater cooperation among governmental branches than seen in the past
  • Mandate public health orders based on medical science rather than previously seen personal thoughts and feelings
  • Focus on the people over the economy

Taxation Of The Ultra Rich

A lot of people may have concerns as to how a party can provide all these services and how they can possibly pay for all these challenges. To this we say we must make those who have the power to make our nation better, do so.


The wealthy need to pay as much their share as everyone else. It is not the working class people's responsibility to prop up the wealthy, rather it is the wealthy elites responsibility to ensure their workers lives endure hardship.

  • Introduce a wealth tax focused on taxation brackets of wealth starting in excess of $250,000 and up
  • Close tax loopholes to prevent accounting abuses
  • Introduce government controlled contact-free audit and accounting programs for the private sector so businesses cannot hide & horde money in off shore bank accounts
  • Introduce a high level tax on wealth inheritance from parent or guardian to their child, greater than two million dollars
  • Increase property tax based on number of homes over a primary and secondary estate per family
  • Create an annually higher bracket carbon tax targeting individuals with net worth greater than two million dollars
  • Charge an annual fee to private sector businesses which refuse to operate cooperatively with union controls
